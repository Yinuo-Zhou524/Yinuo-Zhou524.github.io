# Research

My research focuses on tree physiology and forest health, especially how woody tissues respond to environmental stress.

## Bark water vapor conductance

I study variation in bark water vapor conductance among tree species and across environmental conditions. This work examines how bark structure, including lenticels and bark thickness, influences water loss through stems.

## Temperature responses of bark conductance

A major part of my work investigates how bark water vapor conductance changes across temperature gradients and whether these responses differ among species and seasons.

## Flooding and lenticel function

I am also interested in how flooding affects lenticel morphology and bark gas exchange, including the development of hypertrophied lenticels and their consequences for bark permeability.

## Tree stress and mortality

My broader research connects bark-level processes to whole-plant water balance, stress tolerance, and mechanisms contributing to tree decline and mortality.
